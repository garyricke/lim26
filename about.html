01 — Mission

Share the Gospel. Proclaim the Kingdom.

Lutheran Indian Ministries shares the Gospel of Jesus Christ with Native American people, encouraging them to proclaim Christ's Kingdom to their own and to others. We identify, recruit, and prepare Native Christian leaders — and nurture healing within communities.

02 — Vision

All Nations Walking Together in Light.

Every nation, tribe and people comes to know and experience the Father's love — receiving forgiveness, recognizing God-given worth, thriving in healthy communities, and celebrating the unique cultural heritage God gave them in Christ.

03 — Core Belief

Love Is an Action, Not an Emotion.

We listen before we speak. We walk beside and build relationships rooted in trust, not transactions. If Native people cannot see Jesus through our actions, they will never listen to our words. We arrive by invitation — guided by tribal protocol and cultural humility.

Who We Are

Native-Led. Relationship-First.

Founded in 1985, Lutheran Indian Ministries is an independent nonprofit with no denominational funding — entirely supported by people who believe in this work. We serve Native American and Alaska Native communities across the United States.

LIM is led by Native people who know the stories, the traditions, the culture, and the humor. With 574 federally recognized tribes — 229 in Alaska alone — we travel by small plane, boat, and snowmachine to reach communities wherever they are. We don't show up with answers. We arrive with open hearts.

  • We go by invitation only — guided by tribal protocol and cultural humility
  • We don't bring a model — we build relationships, and that changes everything
  • We develop Native leaders to share the Gospel in their own language and culture
  • Audited financials published annually — full transparency and accountability

How We Serve

Three Pillars of Ministry

Everything LIM does flows from three deeply integrated commitments — each reinforcing the others in a whole-life approach to Gospel ministry.

Pillar 01

Proclaim

Sharing the Gospel in culturally grounded ways that honor Native traditions as vessels of God's grace. God speaks in Native tongues to Native hearts. We travel by small plane, boat, and snowmachine — going by invitation, guided by tribal protocol.

Pillar 02

Disciple

Developing Native leaders through campus ministry at Haskell Indian Nations University — serving students from 130+ tribes. A home away from home: meals, Bible studies, mentorship, and the reminder that you don't have to change to be loved by God.

Pillar 03

Heal

A Bible-based small group program addressing the wounds of historical trauma — boarding schools, family separation, cultural erasure, and generations of loss. The curriculum reaches reservations, villages, urban centers, and online across Native communities.

Our History

40 Years of Walking Alongside.

Lutheran Indian Ministries began in 1985 — first as the Lutheran Association of Missionaries and Pilots, renamed in 2010 to better reflect the people and communities at the heart of this work. For four decades, we have walked with Native people not as outsiders, but as partners, learners, and friends.

1884

Haskell's roots. Haskell Indian Nations University founded as a boarding school during forced assimilation. Today a federally funded university — and a symbol of cultural resilience serving students from 100+ tribes.

1985

Founded as the Lutheran Association of Missionaries and Pilots — a ministry rooted in reaching remote Native communities, traveling by small plane across Alaska and the rural West.

2010

Renamed to Lutheran Indian Ministries — more accurately reflecting the breadth of Native communities served and the relational, people-first nature of the work.

Today

Active in 574 tribal communities — proclaiming the Gospel, discipling leaders at Haskell, and bringing healing groups to reservations, villages, urban centers, and online.
