"I know that there were three questions that Justin had sent us. I totally forgot everything, unless there's a paper, what God did, what did God do for that class for you? Okay. When this happened to me, I got to see this person walk away with a promotion. They got to walk away with an award. They got to ETS out of the Army with full benefits. It felt like a slap in the face. And this class really when I needed people the most to sit there with me and to grieve with me as believers, we're so excited to preach the word of God. There's something that happens every single day to one of us. And you know the persons to your left and to your right — you never know. Walking through and as much as you love sometimes scripture, like Carlos said, shelving scripture down somebody's throat is not always the best thing. Sometimes it's just, Hey, let's get a coffee. Participant 3
Lieutenant, U.S. Army

"So I came into this course being brand new to Fort Wainwright. I got here not too long ago. I knew nobody personally, I only knew one name walking into this course. I was very nervous, didn't really know what to expect. I got actually approved for the course the night before. I knew the title. That was it.

I didn't realize going into this course how much it was actually going to impact me. The course taught a lot about trauma, healing trauma in yourself, and then helping others. For me, that was a big thing. I'm in the process of healing through my own trauma right now, and as a lieutenant in the Army, I know in the future I will have soldiers come to me that are going through some form of traumatic events. So I took this course very seriously.

What God did for me — he changed my perspective. I got to understand the trauma I went through and the experience I was in, healing, and I got to be able to learn how to help others. I got a lot more comfortable, and I know I'm not alone here now.

What changed? I definitely changed. I changed a lot. My mentality has changed. I learned that I wasn't broken, that there was healing to be done and that it was possible. And I learned how to step into the word to help with that healing, and it has changed my life already. It's been — what — a week? Changed my life already.

And then we talk about the invitation to others. For me being a new lieutenant here, I don't know very many people, but the few people I have talked to about this course said they were super interested in wanting to come. And in the future, they plan on coming just so they could hear more about what this course could do. I hope that I can help them get here so they can learn to walk the same way that I am. I have a community here now."

Participant 4
Infantry · 1-24 · Suicide Prevention

"So I went to the course with Felipe and Charlotte and Kelsey. It was a good course because you get to understand what people go through. Obviously we're in Alaska and Fairbanks. And one of the things in the briefs that is being told to us is that the suicide rate is very high.

And it comes from understanding — not the portion of suicide, because that's the end result — it comes from all the way in, all the steps, and understanding different things that may trigger people: trauma, so on and so forth. I'll give a prime example. I'm in 1-24 Infantry and we had a suicide just before — JP Garcia with Staff Sergeant Arnold. People weren't able to identify the signs and it was so new here and he took his own life. And unfortunately, that's something we have to live with and it's a pain we remember every single day.

Just recently there was — I think it was one or two suicide attempts — that somebody intervened in, and luckily they were able to save their life. So taking this course and understanding different people, understanding how trauma works, what you can do to identify it, and understanding how to deal with it, how to understand people — and just going through that course, being vulnerable makes you open up a little bit so that when somebody's sharing, they don't feel like it's just them releasing.

I felt it helped connect. It's almost like a missing piece in between trying to help somebody steer away from that direction when it comes to trauma, the missing peace, God, and then preventing suicide. So I found it very helpful.

It doesn't take a counselor, a chaplain, or somebody else to help prevent an incident from happening. It could be — God can contest any one of us to be that person for someone. So yeah."
