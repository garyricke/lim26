**\[Mission, Vision & Core Belief\]  “Mission”**

Our mission is simple: Lutheran Indian Ministries shares the Gospel of Jesus Christ with Native people, encouraging them to proclaim Christ’s Kingdom.

But we don’t stop there. We train Native leaders, nurture healing, and witness Christ-centered growth.

Our vision? That one day, all nations will walk together in the light of Christ—restored, thriving, and share the cultural heritage God gave them.

At our core is this belief: all healing comes from God. And for us, Love is an action, not **just** an emotion. 

It’s why we listen before we speak. 

Why we walk beside and build relationships that are rooted in trust, not transactions.  
---

**\[Who We Serve & Why We Are Uniquely Qualified\] Who**

With 574 federally recognized tribes across the United States—229 in Alaska alone—the diversity is astounding—a living portrait woven by God. 

Each community has its own language, customs, and geography. 

We travel by small plane, boats, snowmachines — whatever it takes to reach them.

Our ministry is led by Native people. We know the stories. The traditions, the culture. The humor. All are welcome.

We don’t show up with answers. 

We arrive with open hearts, by invitation, guided by tribal protocol and cultural humility in sharing the Gospel.

We don’t bring a model. We build relationships. And that changes everything.

**\[Healing the Wounds of Trauma\] “Healing”**

For generations, Native communities have carried the weight of trauma—boarding schools, family separation, cultural erasure, and other forms of abuse.

Healing the Wounds of Trauma is one of the  most impactful ministries we offer.

Implementing the American Bible Society’s model, healing groups are led by Native facilitators, and grounded in Biblical principles and Scripture.

Healing groups teach how God views trauma, and his compassion for those experiencing grief, loss, heart wounds and ultimately how God heals and restores. Participants also learn how they can help others.

We don’t promise quick fixes. But we create safe spaces where people can gather, share, and heal while holding onto the hope that only God’s Word can give.

As one participant said, ‘This was the first time I felt God’s Word speak to my pain.’ 

That is why we do this work.

We go where the need is: reservations, villages, urban and rural settings to share how God’s word heals and restores. We are training and equipping native facilitators to hold healing groups in their own communities leading and sharing at the local level. 

God is also moving in campus ministry at Haskell Indian Nations University

**\[Leo and Pola’s Story – A Heart Opened by God’s Word\] “Bible”**

Let me share Leo’s story.

Leo is Keetowah Cherokee and Western **Shoshone**, a Haskell student from Reno, Nevada. 

Leo visited our campus center where he met Pola Farve— Haskell LIGHT Director, and a Haskell graduate herself. 

Pola leads with a cultural understanding, creating a space where students feel safe, and loved. 

Where they can ask questions, eat a home-cooked meal, and explore faith without pressure.

One day, Leo asked Pola for a Bible. Touched by the moment, he shared it on social media.

Leo began reading—and he didn’t stop. Today, Leo is walking a new path, guided by faith.

Haskell LIGHT is more than a ministry. It’s a community. It’s a place where God is moving—one student, one Bible, one relationship at a time.
