Section 01

Brand Essence & Philosophy

The LIM brand is anchored in the "Christ, the Bridge" philosophy — visually and narratively connecting Native American iconography with Christian symbols. Every design decision flows from this theological and relational core.

Proclaim

Share the Gospel in culturally grounded ways — through oral tradition, storytelling, and visual metaphors that honor Native epistemologies. Jesus is not foreign to Native hearts; He speaks their language.

Gospel Culture Story

Disciple

Equip the next generation of Indigenous leaders through programs like Haskell LIGHT at Haskell Indian Nations University — a home away from home for students from 130+ tribes.

Haskell LIGHT Leadership

Heal

Address historical trauma, addiction, and grief through biblically-grounded healing programs. The "Healing the Wounds of Trauma" program provides safe, culturally sensitive spaces for recovery.

Trauma-Informed Recovery

Trauma-Informed Design (TID) Principles

Every visual and UX decision must function as a Safe Harbor for seekers navigating pain. These principles are non-negotiable across all touchpoints.

Safety & Predictability

Consistent navigation, no jarring pop-ups, low-arousal color use. Users in crisis must never feel surprised or trapped.

Empowerment & Choice

Content warnings before trauma testimonies. "Quick Exit" button on sensitive pages. Accessibility controls for text size and contrast.

Cultural Respect

Native identity is not abandoned at the door. Design elements honor the circle, the land, oral tradition, and tribal diversity.

Collaboration

Peer support elements — prayer walls, feedback loops, community story sharing — affirm that no one heals alone.

Logo System

The LIM logo system combines the Medicine Wheel mark — featuring arrowheads pointing in the four sacred directions — with Barlow Condensed typography in Charcoal (#282924). Six variations serve different contexts.

Horizontal Stacked Primary

The official primary logo. Best balance for the long organizational name. Use this version whenever space allows — headers, letterheads, presentations, and all primary brand touchpoints.

Horizontal Stacked (Alternate)

Use on stone-gray or light warm backgrounds. Maintain Charcoal text treatment.

On Dark Background Use This File

Use lutheranindianMinistries-logo-horz-stacked-2026-over-dark-background.svg on Charcoal, LIM Black, or dark photography. This file has white text built in — never apply CSS filters or inversion to the standard logo.

Horizontal Single Line

Use in narrow horizontal spaces: website top bar at small sizes, email footers, document headers.

Vertical / Stacked

Use for square-format applications: social media profiles, print materials with limited width, event badges.

Square

Use for equal-dimension placements: app icons, favicon, social media profile photos, merchandise.

Brand Mark Only

Use only when the full name appears nearby in context — favicons, watermarks, social media icons, or as a decorative element after brand recognition is established.

Clear Space & Minimum Size

Always maintain a minimum clear space equal to the cap-height of the letter "L" on all sides. Never place other elements, text, or imagery within this zone.

Section 05

Voice & Messaging

LIM speaks to two distinct audiences simultaneously. All messaging must bridge the Seeker (Native community members seeking healing) and the Donor (primarily Lutheran supporters stewarding resources for the mission).

Donor Path — "Fuel the Mission"

  • Tone: Trustworthy, transparent, mission-driven
  • Lead with impact outcomes ("$35 = one student's Bible & study materials")
  • Emphasize "Native-Led" as a trust differentiator
  • Reference Charity Navigator rating and audited financials
  • Language: "Stewardship," "Gospel," "Mission," "Faithful Partner"
  • CTA Color: LIM Red — high urgency, high contrast
  • Show financial transparency before asking for gifts

Seeker Path — "Find Healing"

  • Tone: Warm, safe, non-institutional, relational
  • Never say "you need to change your identity"
  • Lead with belonging: "You don't have to stop being Native"
  • Prioritize "Healing Groups," crisis resources, local contacts
  • Language: "Journey," "Healing," "Community," "Welcome Home"
  • CTA Color: Sky Blue / Sage — calm, inviting, non-urgent
  • Trauma warnings before sensitive content

Tone Principles

Relational, Not Institutional

Speak person-to-person. Avoid bureaucratic language. Use first and second person.

Hopeful, Not Preachy

Share truth with grace. Avoid condescension or urgency that creates anxiety.

Specific, Not Generic

Name real people, real places (Haskell, Billings, Anchorage), real outcomes. Numbers matter.

Culturally Grounded

Honor oral tradition, storytelling, and visual metaphor. Avoid the "savior" narrative entirely.

Section 07

Digital & UX Standards

Digital touchpoints must balance high-performance donor conversion with a trauma-informed, low-arousal experience for seekers. These standards are binding for all web, email, and digital asset production.

Imagery Standards

  • Authentic photography only — no stock images of Native people
  • Warm color grade on all photos (reduce cool tones)
  • Faces of real students, elders, and healing circle participants
  • Circle motif (Medicine Wheel) for image frames and icons
  • Avoid "trauma porn" — no exploitative suffering imagery
  • Obtain full consent and release for all community photography

Navigation Strategy

  • Dual-path navigation: Seeker vs. Donor clearly differentiated
  • "Need Help?" in Sky Blue — always above the fold
  • "Donate" in LIM Red — right-side of nav, always visible
  • Max 5 items in primary navigation
  • Sticky header on scroll for persistent access
  • No jarring popups — use inline smart nudges only

Performance & Accessibility

  • Target: <3s page load on 3G (reservation mobile users)
  • Serve WebP images with lazy loading
  • All interactive elements keyboard accessible
  • All images have descriptive alt text
  • Video: captions required; transcripts for all audio
  • WCAG 2.1 AA minimum across all pages

Donation & Giving

  • Embedded giving — never redirect to external processor
  • Apple Pay / Google Pay enabled (reduces friction to <10s)
  • Default to Monthly giving; gently nudge one-time donors
  • Remove non-essential form fields (no County, Organization)
  • Dedicated "Smart Giving" page for stocks, DAFs, crypto
  • Display Charity Navigator & GuideStar seals near donate button

Sensitive Content & TID

  • Content warnings overlay before trauma/abuse testimonies
  • "Quick Exit" button (redirects to Google) on crisis pages
  • Crisis hotlines visible on all healing-related pages
  • No autoplay video with sound on any page
  • Avoid red backgrounds on healing/crisis sections
  • Offer text-size and contrast accessibility controls

SEO & Content Strategy

  • Target: "Native American trauma healing," "Indigenous Christian ministry"
  • Avoid reliance on generic scripture verse keywords
  • Podcast transcripts required for all Sacred Connections episodes
  • Healing group pages indexed by location (Billings, Anchorage, etc.)
  • GA4 conversion events: Donations, Signups, PDF Downloads
  • Cookiebot for GDPR/CCPA compliance

Section 08

Do's & Don'ts

These rules protect the brand's integrity and cultural respect. Violations dilute trust with both Native communities and donors.

Logo Usage

Do

  • Use the provided SVG files at all times for crisp rendering
  • Maintain clear space equal to the cap-height of "L" on all sides
  • Use -over-dark-background.svg on dark backgrounds — it has white text built in
  • Use the primary horizontal stacked version as the default
  • Use only Charcoal (#282924) for logo text in light contexts
  • Scale proportionally — never stretch or compress

Don't

  • Never recreate the logo in a different typeface
  • Never place the logo on a busy photographic background without overlay
  • Never use LIM Yellow as the text color for the wordmark
  • Never apply CSS filters or color inversion to any logo file
  • Never add drop shadows, glows, or 3D effects
  • Never rotate or distort the mark
  • Never use the mark alone if the full name hasn't been established

Color Usage

Do

  • Use LIM Red sparingly — as a high-signal action color only
  • Pair LIM Yellow with Charcoal text for WCAG-compliant readability
  • Use Sage Green and Sky Blue for healing-focused content sections
  • Use Stone Gray for long-form reading backgrounds
  • Allow generous White space throughout all layouts

Don't

  • Never use LIM Red as a background on trauma/healing pages
  • Never use LIM Yellow as text on a white background (fails contrast)
  • Never use LIM Black for body text — use Charcoal instead
  • Never introduce off-brand colors (e.g., purples, teals, oranges)
  • Never use high-contrast red/green color pairings (accessibility)

Imagery & Cultural Representation

Do

  • Use authentic photography of actual LIM communities and students
  • Apply warm color grading to all imagery
  • Feature faces of Native elders, leaders, and youth prominently
  • Obtain signed photo consent and releases for all people shown
  • Frame images with the circle motif when used decoratively
  • Show joy, community, belonging, and resilience

Don't

  • Never use stock photos of generic Native Americans
  • Never use historical or archival imagery of boarding schools without explicit context
  • Never use exploitative "poverty" imagery ("trauma porn")
  • Never show ceremonial or sacred cultural items without tribal permission
  • Never position Native people as passive recipients — show agency
  • Never use headdresses, dreamcatchers, or pan-tribal stereotypes
